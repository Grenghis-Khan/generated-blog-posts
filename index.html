Key Milestones in Psychological Assessment and Diagnosis in 2025

Published Sep 10, 2025

Key Points

  • The Examination for Professional Practice in Psychology (EPPP) celebrates its 60th anniversary in 2025, marking six decades as the gold standard for psychology licensure in the U.S. and Canada.
  • In 2024, over 8,000 candidates took the EPPP, reflecting its critical role in ensuring psychometrically sound and fair licensure.
  • ASPPB is developing a new single-session competence-based version of the EPPP for 2025, aiming for more efficient, integrated evaluation methods.
  • The Assessment Institute at Indiana University remains a vital annual event for advancing assessment knowledge, primarily in educational contexts.
  • Scientific advancements, supported by the National Institute of Mental Health, continue in 2025 to refine diagnostic tools for disorders like bipolar disorder and suicide risk, contributing to ongoing improvement in psychological assessment and treatment.

A major celebration in professional psychology in 2025 is the 60th anniversary of the Examination for Professional Practice in Psychology (EPPP), established by the Association of State and Provincial Psychology Boards (ASPPB). Since its inception in 1965, the EPPP has become the gold standard for assessing the readiness of psychologists to practice in the United States and Canada, currently used by 66 member jurisdictions. In 2024 alone, over 8,000 candidates took the exam, underlining its critical role in public protection by ensuring fair, consistent, and psychometrically sound licensure processes[1].

This anniversary highlights six decades of commitment to rigorous professional assessment in psychology, specifically addressing competence in diagnosing and managing neurodevelopmental, neurobehavioral, cognitive, mood, and personality disorders. The EPPP's evolution reflects the field's dedication not only to maintaining high standards in licensure but also to adapting to contemporary needs. For 2025, the ASPPB is developing a single-session competence-based EPPP, signifying a shift toward more efficient and integrated evaluation methods while preserving psychometric reliability and fairness[1].

Additionally, the Assessment Institute at Indiana University, celebrating its standing as the oldest and largest higher education event focused on assessment and improvement, continues to provide a vital forum for advancing assessment practices, although its primary focus is on educational assessment rather than clinical diagnostics[3]. This event, held annually, offers extensive opportunities for professionals involved in assessment and measurement, including those working within psychology and related fields, to engage in knowledge exchange and skill enhancement.

In the broader context of mental health and psychological diagnostics, ongoing scientific advancements funded by institutions like the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) are contributing to refined diagnostic tools and better understanding of disorders such as bipolar disorder, memory-related conditions, and suicide risk. While not an anniversary, 2025 sees these advances highlighted as part of the continuous evolution of psychological assessment and treatment techniques[2].

Together, these milestones and ongoing initiatives emphasize the dynamic history and forward momentum of institutions dedicated to psychological assessment and diagnosis, celebrating both legacy and innovation in the pursuit of effective mental health care.
